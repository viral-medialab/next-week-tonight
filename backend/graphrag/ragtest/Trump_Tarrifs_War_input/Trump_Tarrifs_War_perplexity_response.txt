## Extensive News Coverage of the Trump Tariff War

The Trump administration's tariff policies, commonly referred to as the "Trump Tariff War," have been extensively covered by major news outlets, academic institutions, and reputable media organizations. The following sources provide both historical context and recent updates, capturing a wide range of perspectives and developments across different time periods.

### Timeline and Key Events

- PBS News provides a comprehensive timeline of Trump's tariff actions, detailing the escalation of tariffs on steel, aluminum, and a wide array of goods from China, the European Union, South Korea, Japan, and Taiwan. The coverage outlines both the imposition of U.S. tariffs and the retaliatory measures enacted by China, Canada, and the EU. Recent updates include Trump’s decision to postpone certain tariffs and declare a national economic emergency to justify sweeping import taxes[1].

### Impact and Reactions

- CBS News offers a detailed list of the reciprocal tariffs implemented by the Trump administration, affecting about 90 countries. Their reporting highlights which countries face standard versus elevated tariff rates, the administration's justification for these tariffs, and forecasts from economists on their potential inflationary effects and risk of international retaliation. The analysis notes that Asian nations in particular face the harshest tariff hikes[3].

### Ongoing Analysis and Criticism

- The New Republic explores recent developments and criticisms surrounding the tariff strategy, referencing an extensive New York Times analysis indicating that Trump’s aggressive tariffs have thus far failed to produce significant concessions from China. This piece underscores ongoing concerns about the efficacy and consequences of the tariff war[2].

### Themes Across Coverage

- Escalating tit-for-tat tariff measures between the U.S. and major trading partners, most notably China and the EU[1][2][3].
- Significant impacts on global supply chains, inflation concerns, and risks of broader trade conflicts[2][3].
- Political and legislative responses, including rare bipartisan pushback against tariffs that affected allied nations such as Canada[1].
- Shifting strategies, such as the announcement of a universal import tariff with specific higher levies for certain countries, reflecting evolving U.S. trade policy under Trump[3].
