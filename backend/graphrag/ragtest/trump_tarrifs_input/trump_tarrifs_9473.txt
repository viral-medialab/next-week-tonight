The Tax Foundation is a nonpartisan, nonprofit tax policy research organization, globally recognized as a leading authority on sound tax policy. For over 85 years, the organization has focused on promoting tax policies that are simple, neutral, transparent, and stable, aiming to foster economic growth and provide greater opportunities. Its mission is to improve lives through research and education on tax policy, while its vision is centered on a world where tax codes do not hinder success.

### Areas of Focus
The Tax Foundation is structured into three main research centers:
1. **Center for Federal Tax Policy**: Analyzes U.S. federal tax issues and their global comparison.
2. **Center for State Tax Policy**: Provides insights into state tax structures and reforms.
3. **Center for Global Tax Policy**: Promotes policies facilitating global growth and presents comparative analysis of international tax systems.

### Activities and Contributions
- **Research and Publications**: The organization produces studies such as the *State Business Tax Climate Index* and the *International Tax Competitiveness Index*. They also analyze legislative tax proposals and their economic impacts using their "Taxes and Growth" macroeconomic model.
- **Education through TaxEDU**: Offers resources to improve tax literacy via tools, educational content, and classroom-ready materials for a better understanding of tax policies.
- **Data Accessibility**: Presents foundational data on taxes, spending, and taxation statistics since its inception, helping policymakers, educators, and citizens alike.

### Historical Highlights
- Founded in 1937 in New York City, the Tax Foundation initially aimed to monitor government tax and spending policies.
- It became instrumental during World War II by promoting fiscal restraint to support war financing.
- In 1978, it fully relocated to Washington, D.C., further deepening its influence on federal tax policy.

The organization continues to play a critical role in shaping tax debates, offering data-driven insights and advocating for reforms that align with its core principles.
